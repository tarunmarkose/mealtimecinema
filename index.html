Lock them into the app

So they can't accidentally swipe away to YouTube or another app. One-time setup, then just a triple-click at mealtime.

Step 1 of 2

Turn on Guided Access

  1. Open Settings → tap Accessibility
  2. Scroll down to the General section → tap Guided Access
  3. Toggle Guided Access to ON
  4. Tap Passcode Settings → Set Guided Access Passcode → choose a 6-digit code (kids can't guess it, but you'll remember it)
  5. Toggle Accessibility Shortcut to ON — this makes the triple-click work
Step 2 of 2

Use it at mealtime

  1. Open Eat & Watch from the home screen
  2. Triple-click the side button (top button on iPad without home button) or the home button (older iPads)
  3. Tap Start in the top right
  4. iPad is now locked to this app. To exit: triple-click again, enter your passcode, tap End.
💡 Tip: While in Guided Access you can also draw a circle around any part of the screen with your finger to disable taps in that region — useful for blocking the YouTube logo inside the player.
Step 1 of 2

Turn on App Pinning

  1. Open Settings → Security (or Security & Privacy)
  2. Tap More security settings if shown
  3. Find App pinning (or Screen pinning) → toggle ON
  4. Toggle Ask for PIN before unpinning ON
Step 2 of 2

Use it at mealtime

  1. Open Eat & Watch from the home screen
  2. Swipe up from the bottom and hold to see recent apps
  3. Tap the app's icon at the top of its card
  4. Tap Pin (or Pin this app)
  5. To exit: swipe up and hold, then enter your PIN
💡 Tip: Older Android versions: tap the square recent-apps button instead of swiping up. Some Samsung devices call this "Pin window" instead.
